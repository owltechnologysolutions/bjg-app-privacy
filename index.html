2. What this policy covers

This policy describes the personal information BJG collects through the Golf Ranch App (iOS and Android) and how we use it. It does not cover:

  • Information BJG collects in person at a Golf Ranch site (front-desk membership records, point-of-sale purchases without the app).
  • Information collected by the BJG website, marketing emails, or other channels — those are governed by separate notices on the relevant property.
  • Third-party services we link to from the app — those have their own privacy policies, listed in §6 below.

If you have a Ranch Pass and don't use the app, this policy doesn't apply to you; your membership data is governed by BJG's general member privacy notice.

3. Information we collect

3.a Information you give us directly

  • Email address. Used to create your account and authenticate you. We never see your password — authentication is handled by our third-party identity provider (Supabase), which stores only a hashed value.
  • Home site selection. The Golf Ranch location you've chosen as your primary site. V1 supports Richardson only.

3.b Information we receive from BJG's existing membership system

When you sign in, the app verifies you are a current Ranch Pass member. To do this, we look up your email in BJG's existing membership data warehouse and read:

  • Your first and last name (as on your Ranch Pass membership).
  • Your membership status (active, lapsed).
  • Your member-since date.

This data is sourced from BJG's existing membership system; no new collection happens at sign-up beyond the email and home site you provide.

3.c Information generated by your use of the app

  • Purchase history. When you buy a ball bucket through the app, we record the date, time, site, ball count, price paid, and a transaction identifier. This drives your loyalty stats, visit history, and tier progression.
  • PIN issuance and redemption. When you purchase a bucket, the app obtains a one-time PIN code from SelectPi (the kiosk operator) so you can dispense balls. We record the PIN's issuance time, expiration, and whether it was redeemed at the kiosk.
  • Loyalty stats. Aggregated counts derived from your purchases — total balls hit, savings versus walk-in pricing, weekly streak, and tier progress — calculated server-side and surfaced in the app's Stats tab.

3.d Information collected automatically

  • Crash and error reports. If the app crashes or hits an unexpected error, we collect a stack trace and high-level device information (device model, OS version, app version) to diagnose the issue. We do not collect screenshots, keystrokes, or the contents of your data.
  • Authentication events. Sign-in attempts, sign-outs, and password resets are logged for security and abuse-prevention purposes.
  • Product analytics. We record events describing how you move through the app — for example, which step of the signup flow you reached, whether a purchase completed, whether a kiosk PIN was redeemed — along with high-level device information (platform, app version) and a randomly-generated anonymous session identifier stored on your device. This helps us identify where users get stuck, diagnose flow-level bugs, and prioritize improvements. We do not use third-party analytics or advertising SDKs; events are sent only to our own servers and may include error-message text from underlying services that, in rare cases, can incidentally reference your email. Analytics events are retained for thirteen months.

3.e What we do NOT collect

The V1 app does not collect:

  • Card numbers, expiration dates, CVCs, or any other payment-card data. All payments are handled directly by Stripe; card details are entered into Stripe's payment sheet and never touch BJG or OTS systems. We see only an opaque payment identifier and a confirmation that the charge succeeded.
  • Your location (GPS, Wi-Fi, or otherwise). The app does not request location permissions.
  • Contact-list, photo-library, microphone, or camera data. The app does not request access to any of these.
  • Behavioral / advertising trackers. The app contains no third-party analytics SDKs or advertising SDKs.
  • Children's information. The app is intended for adult Ranch Pass members; we do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 13. If you believe a minor has created an account, contact us at the email above and we will remove it.

4. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Provide the service — authenticate you, verify your Ranch Pass membership, present member-priced bucket purchases, generate kiosk PINs, and keep an accurate record of your loyalty stats.
  • Process payments — pass charge requests to Stripe, our payment processor.
  • Detect and prevent abuse — rate-limit suspicious sign-in attempts, enforce one-active-pass-per-user, and investigate fraud or unauthorized use.
  • Improve reliability — diagnose crashes and bugs.
  • Comply with legal obligations — respond to lawful requests from law enforcement or regulators.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your information for advertising purposes. We do not share your information with marketers.

5. Legal basis for processing (EU/UK readers)

Where the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation applies, we rely on:

  • Performance of a contract — to provide the app's purchase, PIN, and stats functionality you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests — to detect abuse, diagnose errors, and run the service securely. These interests do not override your fundamental rights.
  • Consent — for any future feature that explicitly asks for it (none in V1).
  • Legal obligation — for tax, accounting, or law-enforcement compliance.

9. Security

We use industry-standard measures to protect your data:

  • All traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+).
  • Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256.
  • Authentication tokens are stored in your device's secure keychain (iOS Keychain / Android EncryptedSharedPreferences), never in plain storage.
  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt by our identity provider; we never see your password in plaintext.
  • Access to production data inside BJG and OTS is restricted to the engineers who need it, with audit logs on every access.

No system is perfectly secure; if a breach affecting your information ever occurs, we will notify you in line with applicable law.

10. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access — get a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Correction — fix data that's wrong (most fields can be corrected through the app's Profile tab; for membership-system fields, contact BJG directly).
  • Deletion — delete your account and associated data.
  • Portability — receive a machine-readable export.
  • Objection / restriction — object to certain processing or ask us to limit it.
  • Withdraw consent — where we rely on consent (none in V1), withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at the address in §1. We respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before acting on the request.

If you live in the EU/UK, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority (e.g. the UK ICO, Ireland's DPC).

If you live in California, the CCPA/CPRA gives you these same rights (under different names) and additionally the right not to be discriminated against for exercising them. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.
